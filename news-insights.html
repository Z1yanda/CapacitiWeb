CAPACITI Receives R15M Government Funding to Expand Digital Skills Training

Cape Town, South Africa - The Department of Higher Education and Training has awarded CAPACITI a groundbreaking R15 million funding package to dramatically expand digital skills training across South Africa.

This significant investment will enable CAPACITI to train an additional 1,000 students in 2025, representing a 200% increase in our current capacity. The funding will support the establishment of new training centers in Durban and Port Elizabeth, bringing our comprehensive digital skills programmes closer to underserved communities.

Programme Expansion Details

The funding will specifically target:

  • Advanced Technical Academy programmes in DevOps, Java, and Data Analytics
  • Women in Business technology training initiatives
  • Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) certification programmes
  • Creative industry skills development

"This funding represents a vote of confidence in CAPACITI's proven track record of transforming lives through digital skills training," said CEO Jane Smith. "We're excited to bring our programmes to new communities and help bridge South Africa's digital skills gap."

The programme is expected to launch in March 2025, with applications opening in February. Priority will be given to candidates from previously disadvantaged backgrounds and rural communities.
